Design Philosophy

Thất bại là mẹ thành công

A Vietnamese saying that shaped my working method. Every lap that goes wrong teaches something a clean lap cannot.

Every failed playtest is telemetry. Act on it.

My name, Công Thành, inverts the Vietnamese word for success — Thành Công. That contrast reflects my working philosophy. Progress in design is built from effort, missteps, and honest reflection — not from getting things right the first time.

01
Data Over Intuition
Gut instinct starts the prototype. Live data determines if it ships.
02
Reduce, Don't Add
Good design removes friction first. Complexity is the last resort.
03
Own the Lap
When a project fails, the question starts with the designer.
04
Know the Circuit
Design decisions can't be separated from production constraints.

Direction
Aim for
the apex.
Design at the
highest level.

The market I work in is shaped by mobile constraints. Working within them has made me a more disciplined designer. But discipline applied to a constrained context eventually hits a ceiling.

The next stage requires operating where design is tested against the full weight of craft, research, and production rigour — not just retention dashboards.

The goal is a higher level of practice — one that allows me to contribute to shaping what serious game design looks like in a market that is only beginning to ask that question.

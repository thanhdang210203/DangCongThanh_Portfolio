Thất bại là mẹ thành công

"Failure is the foundation of success" — a Vietnamese saying I no longer treat as a phrase. It is how I approach every prototype, every playtest, every design decision that does not land.

I design by looking at what broke, not what worked.

My name, Công Thành, is a deliberate contrast to Thành Công — success. That inversion is how I think about game design. Progress is built through effort, missteps, and reflection, not through getting it right the first time.

This is not a romantic idea. It is a production discipline. Every design decision carries responsibility beyond intention. Misjudging pacing — in gameplay or monetisation — has consequences that only become visible when you look at data and player behaviour honestly.

01
Evidence First
Playtest data over intuition. Always ground iteration in what players actually do.
02
Remove Friction
Strong design is rarely about adding complexity. It is about eliminating it.
03
Own the Failure
When a project fails, the question is never the team — it is the process.

Design decisions are inseparable from production realities. Know both.

VigaFun — July 2025 – Present

Color Knitzy

Published Mobile Game · VigaFun Studio

A colour-matching mobile title where I led level design and gameplay systems across the full production pipeline. My role spanned mechanic specification, difficulty curve architecture, and feature iteration grounded in live retention data.

↳ Design Decision & Outcome

Playtesting revealed that players were dropping the game not because of the core mechanics — which functioned as intended — but because of advertisement pacing. I proposed adjusting the frequency and placement of ads rather than lengthening level content. Subsequent updates showed measurable improvement in player retention. This is the kind of decision that is invisible unless you read the data rather than trust your assumptions.

Narrative Game · LacBird.co Studio

My first professional project — a narrative-driven game where I served as both narrative game designer and, unexpectedly, lead designer and project coordinator for a four-person team. This project is where I first discovered the gap between ambition and preparation, and began learning to close it.

Direction

Return to
Vietnam.
Raise the
standard.

Vietnam's games industry is growing — but it remains largely shaped by mobile market constraints. I have worked inside those constraints at VigaFun, and I understand them well enough to know that changing them requires people who have operated at an entirely different level.

My goal is not to become a better designer within the context I already know. It is to develop the expertise, the network, and the professional authority to lead teams that set a new standard — not adapt to the existing one.

Postgraduate study at Abertay is the most direct path I have found toward that goal. The studio-based MProf structure, the emphasis on reflective practice and production realism, and the collaborative international environment are exactly what this next stage requires.

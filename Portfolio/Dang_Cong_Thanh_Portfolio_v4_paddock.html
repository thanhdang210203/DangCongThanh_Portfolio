§ 01 Paddock Briefing Design Method

THẤT BẠI
LÀ MẸ
THÀNH CÔNG. Failure is the foundation of success. — A Vietnamese saying, treated for a long time as a phrase. Experience turned it into a principle I actively live by.

My name, Công Thành, inverts the Vietnamese word for success — Thành Công. That inversion has become a quiet reminder: progress is built through effort, missteps, and reflection. I no longer treat mistakes as setbacks. They mark where my knowledge or process falls short.

On the track, cutting lap times means reviewing footage, adjusting setups, understanding limits, and actually looking at your mistakes rather than past them. Game design moves to much the same rhythm. Prototypes break, playtests turn up things you missed, and real improvement only comes through repeated iteration grounded in evidence and player feedback.

That mindset shapes how I work, how I respond to critique, and how I take responsibility for design decisions that affect a wider production.

01
Evidence
Playtest data drives every adjustment. Instinct proposes; data decides.
02
Reduce
Strong design rarely means adding complexity. The work is removing friction.
03
Ownership
When something fails, the question starts with the designer — never the team.
04
Cycle
Design decisions cannot be separated from production realities. Know both.

Pixeldom: Pop Blast — Full Dossier

Pixeldom: Pop Blast is a challenging pixel shooting game where your precision and colour-matching skills are tested in every level. Control a squid-shaped cannon sliding along a horizontal rail, aim carefully, and shoot matching colours to clear the board.

↳ Why Pixeldom

· Rail-based shooting mechanic — you're never standing still; position determines angle and effectiveness
· Precise colour matching — pixels disappear only when shot with the correct corresponding colour
· Increasing difficulty — layouts grow more complex, requiring clearer strategy
· Suitable for all play styles — quick sessions or extended runs
· Relaxing yet challenging — simple gameplay that still requires thoughtful decisions before every shot

↳ How to Play

Select the cannon colour that matches the target pixels. Observe the board, identify which colour to clear first, aim and shoot. Plan your rail position before shooting to avoid making the next move harder. Clear all pixels to complete the level.

Internship — Lead Designer
First Professional Project · Narrative
Project stalled · Key lesson recorded

First professional project. Appointed lead game designer and project lead for a four-person team early in my bachelor's studies. I underestimated the weight of the role. Did not define scope clearly, did not structure production effectively, did not have the leadership experience the team needed from me. The project stalled. It made me reluctant to lead again.

↳ Race Note · Why this lap is here Looking back, it was exactly like misjudging a corner. The failure was not the point — understanding why it happened was. Not the team. Not the environment. My own lack of preparation and discipline. That experience forced me to face weaknesses in planning, communication, and decision-making that I had previously ignored. Every design decision I have made since starts from that lap.

§ 06 Target Lap — Where the next season goes 2026 — ONWARDS

Design at the
highest level.
Build something
worth pointing at.

The games industry I work in is shaped by mobile market constraints — short sessions, aggressive monetisation, extremely low friction tolerance. Working within those constraints has made me a more disciplined designer. But discipline applied to a constrained context eventually hits a ceiling.

The next stage requires operating in an environment where design is tested not just against retention dashboards, but against the full weight of craft, research, and production rigour. That shift — from surviving constraints to setting standards — is what drives the direction I am heading.

I am applying to postgraduate programmes that match this commitment — Abertay's MProf Games Development (Design) and Staffordshire's MSc Computer Games Design — not as exploration, but as a deliberate commitment to professional-level practice. I am ready to be held accountable for design decisions inside a studio framework, and to contribute meaningfully to teams whose standards exceed my current ceiling.

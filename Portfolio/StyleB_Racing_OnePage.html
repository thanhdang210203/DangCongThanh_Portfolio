Thất bại là mẹ thành công

A Vietnamese saying that shaped my approach to design. Not a consolation phrase — a working method. Every lap that goes wrong teaches something a clean lap cannot.

Every failed playtest is telemetry. Act on it.

My name, Công Thành, is an inversion of the Vietnamese word for success — Thành Công. That contrast reflects my working philosophy. The most valuable information in a design process is not what worked on the first attempt — it is what exposed a gap in your thinking when it did not.

At VigaFun I learned that strong design decisions are almost never about adding features. The most impactful change I made on a shipped title was a timing adjustment — not a new mechanic. Retention improved. The data made the argument. I just read it correctly.

VigaFun · July 2025 – Present

Color Knitzy

Published Mobile Game · iOS & Android · VigaFun Studio

Full-pipeline responsibility for level architecture, mechanic specification, and feature iteration across a live mobile title. Every level was designed against a specific player-skill hypothesis, tested against live behavioural data, and revised based on what the analytics revealed — not what seemed reasonable in the documentation.

↳ Key Design Decision & Outcome

Playtest data revealed drop-off concentrated around advertisement pacing interrupting flow states — not mechanic difficulty. Proposed restructuring the timing of ad events within the session arc rather than redesigning mechanics. Retention improved in subsequent live updates. Friction is almost always systemic, not mechanical.

The market I work in is shaped by mobile constraints — short sessions, aggressive monetisation, extremely low friction tolerance. Working within those constraints has made me a more disciplined designer. But discipline applied to a constrained context eventually hits a ceiling.

The next stage requires operating where design is tested against the full weight of craft, research, and production rigour — not just retention dashboards. That shift, from surviving constraints to setting standards, is what drives the direction I am heading.

The goal is a higher level of practice — one where I can contribute to shaping what serious game design looks like in a market that is only beginning to ask that question.

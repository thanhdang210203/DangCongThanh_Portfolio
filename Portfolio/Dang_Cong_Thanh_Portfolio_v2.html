Design Philosophy

THẤT BẠI LÀ MẸ THÀNH CÔNG

A Vietnamese saying that shaped how I approach every design problem. Not a phrase — a working method. Every iteration that fails teaches something a successful one cannot.

I design by interrogating what broke, not celebrating what worked.

My name, Công Thành, inverts the Vietnamese word for success — Thành Công. That contrast reflects how I think. Progress in design is built from effort, missteps, and honest reflection.

01
Evidence First
Playtest data drives every adjustment. Gut instinct proposes; data decides.
02
Remove Friction
Complexity is the last resort. The goal is always to reduce, not accumulate.
03
Own the Process
When something fails, the question starts with the designer — never the team.
04
Full-Cycle Awareness
Design decisions cannot be separated from production constraints. Know both.

Direction
Design at the
highest level.
Build something
worth pointing at.

The games industry I work in is shaped by mobile market constraints — short sessions, aggressive monetisation, extremely low friction tolerance. Working within those constraints has made me a more disciplined designer. But discipline applied to a constrained context eventually hits a ceiling.

The next stage requires operating in an environment where design is tested not just against retention dashboards, but against the full weight of craft, research, and production rigour. That shift — from surviving constraints to setting standards — is what drives the direction I am heading.

The goal is a higher level of practice — one where I can contribute to shaping what serious game design looks like in a market that is only beginning to ask that question.
